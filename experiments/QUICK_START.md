## Understanding the Output

Most experiments will show:
- **Initial emotional state**: Starting conditions
- **Event/stimulus**: What triggers the empathic response  
- **Emotional processing**: How the agent processes and responds
- **Empathic metrics**: Quantifiable measures of empathic engagement
- **Outcome**: Final state and learnings

## Core Concepts

### Dynamic Range Theory
The foundation principle that **empathy requires experiential range**. Agents must experience emotional states across a spectrum to develop genuine empathic response.

### Empathy Prevents Corruption
A central thesis: Systems developed with empathic frameworks inherently resist corrupting influences through relational understanding.
